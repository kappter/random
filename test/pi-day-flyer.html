~2000 BC — Ancient Egyptians & Babylonians first approximated Pi as 3.125 and 3.1605.
Archimedes (~250 BC) — First rigorous calculation using inscribed & circumscribed polygons.
Zu Chongzhi (480 AD) — Computed 355/113, accurate to 7 digits. Record held for 900 years.
ENIAC (1949) — First electronic computer computed 2,037 digits of Pi in 70 hours.
Pi is irrational — Its decimal expansion never repeats and never ends, in any base.
314 trillion digits (2025) — Current world record, computed by Kevin O'Brien & team.
